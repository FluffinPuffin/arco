Recap

  • Key signature appears next to the treble clef
  • It's a set of up to seven sharps or flats
  • Can't mix sharps and flats in a key signature
  • Key signature tells us which notes are always sharp or flat

Learn about key signatures - the sharps or flats that appear at the beginning of a piece of music.

(0:03 - 0:09) Hi! Welcome to lesson 3.3. In this, we’re going to be talking about key signatures.
(0:09 - 0:20) Next to the treble clef and next to the time signature on a musical staff, we might see symbols like this. This is a key signature.
(0:20 - 0:24) A key signature is responsible for telling us which notes to use with our song.
(0:25 - 0:32) A key signature is a set of up to seven sharps or seven flats. It can also be empty.
(0:32 - 0:39) Both the sharps and the flats follow a specific order when they’re placed on the musical staff.
(0:39 - 0:48) The order of sharps follows this phrase. Fluffy Cats Go Down Alleys Eating Birds.
(0:48 - 0:57) Every first letter of each word lines up with the sharp that shows up in the musical staffs key signature.
(0:58 - 1:13) For example, if I see this key signature in my music, I see three sharps. One, two, three. These sharps line up with the words Fluffy Cats Go. This tells me it will be F sharp, C sharp, and G sharp.
(1:16 - 1:26) The order of flats in a key signature follows the phrase Ben Ellie And Dad Go Catch Fish.
(1:26 - 1:40) For example, if I see a key signature with three flats like this, I'm going to think of my phrase,“Ben Ellie And” and that would give me the flats. B flat, E flat, and A flat.
(1:40 - 1:47) Key signatures help us identify what key we’re playing in, what notes we’re using.
(1:47 - 2:00) For example, if I see a key signature with zero sharps and zero flats, that is the key of C major. This means when I play my song, I will not play any sharps or flats.
(2:01 - 2:11) If I see a key with one sharp, that’s the key of G major. That means I will play F sharp. But every other note is just natural.
(2:12 - 2:27) If I see a key with two sharps, this is the key of D major. This tells me that I have an F sharp and a C sharp. In my song, all F’s are sharp and all C’s are sharp, but all other notes are natural.
(2:27 - 2:39) If my key signature has flats, one flat that means I'm in the key of F major and it means that B has been flattened. All other notes are natural.
(2:39 - 2:50) If I see two flats, that means I am in the key of B flat major. B flat and E flat are the notes that will be flattened and everyone else is natural.
(2:50 - 2:58) It’s very important to memorize these key signatures so that way when you pick up a song you know what notes you’re supposed to be using.
