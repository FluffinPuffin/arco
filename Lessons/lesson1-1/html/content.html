  • Strings from lowest to highest, left to right: G, D, A, E
  • The pegs tune strings, while the bridge holds the strings up
  • Place fingers on fingerboard to play, place chin on chin rest to balance violin

Learn about the parts of the violin including the scroll, pegs, strings, fingerboard, bridge, tail piece, fine tuners, and chin rest.

Welcome to lesson 1! Let’s talk about parts of the violin. This is called the scroll. This part is called the peg box. These are the pegs. The pegs are used to tighten and loosen our strings, like this. If I turn the peg away from me, it tightens the string. If I turn the peg towards me, it loosens the string. There are four strings, from lowest to highest we have G, D, A, and E. Here’s what they sound like. This is G. It’s very low and grumpy. This is D. This is A. And this is E. This part is called the bridge; it holds our strings up. This part is called the fingerboard. This is where we place our fingers when we want to play. You can play with your fingers here or even down here because this entire thing is where the fingers go. This part is the chin rest. This is where we place our chin or even the side of our jaw when we play. Like this. It helps us hold the violin without using our hands.

Recap

  • Bow Parts: Stick, horse hair, tip, frog, eye, screw, winding, grip
  • The stick is made of wood and slightly curved
  • The frog is what most violinists hold onto when they play
  • The screw tightens (right) or loosens (left) the bow hair

Learn about the parts of the bow including the stick, horse hair, tip, frog, eye, screw, winding, and grip.

This is the bow. This is what we use to draw across our strings to create sound, like this. The parts of the bow are called the stick, the hair, the winding, the pad, the frog, and the screw. The stick is made out of wood. The hair is traditionally made out of horse hair. The frog is what most violinists hold onto when they play, it gives us a nice place to grab. And the screw on the end is used to tighten the hair and loosen the hair. We loosen the hair when we’re done by turning the screw left. We tighten the hair when we’re ready to play by turning right. Go ahead and try it out on yours, but be careful not to tighten the hair too much or you can cause the wood to bend permanently!
