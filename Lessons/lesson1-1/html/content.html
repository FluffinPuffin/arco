Learn about the parts of the violin including the scroll, pegs, strings, fingerboard, bridge, tail piece, fine tuners, and chin rest.

Image of violin with parts: scroll, pegs, strings, fingerboard, bridge, tail piece, fine tuners, chin rest The pegs are used for tuning the strings. We can turn them forward and backward to change the note up and down! There are four strings. From lowest to highest, and left to right, the strings are G, D, A, E. The bridge holds our strings up, it also allows vibrations from the strings to travel down into the body of the violin, which comes out of the acoustic holes as sound! The fingerboard is where you place your fingers to change the notes. The chin rest is where you place your chin to help hold the violin.

Learn about the parts of the bow including the stick, horse hair, tip, frog, eye, screw, winding, and grip.

Image of the bow with parts labelled: Stick, horse hair, tip, frog, eye, screw, winding, grip. The stick is made out of wood and slightly curved to absorb weight and pressure. The bow hair is traditionally made out of horse hair! The tip of the bow is curved and comes to a little point. The frog is at the base of the bow, this is what most violinists hold onto when they play! The eye is a little dot, commonly made out of mother of pearl. The metal piece at the bottom end of the bow is a secret screw! When turned right, the bow hair is tightened. When turned left, the hair is loosened. The metal winding protects the bow from the violinist's hand. The soft grip provides a place for the violinist's finger to rest while playing
