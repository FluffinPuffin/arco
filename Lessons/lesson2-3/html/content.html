Learn about the four basic types of rests in music: whole rest, half rest, quarter rest, and eighth rest.

Every note has a silent partner called a "rest". When we rest in music, we pause and stay silent until the rest is over. There are four kinds of basic rests: Whole rest, half rest, quarter rest, and eighth rest. A whole rest is worth four beats. When we are counting we hold for 1 - 2 - 3 - 4. A half rest is worth two beats. When we are counting we hold for 1 - 2. A quarter rest is worth one beat. When we are counting we hold for 1. An eighth rest is worth a half beat.

Recap

  • Half rest looks like a hat (sits on the line)
  • Whole rest looks like a hole in the ground (hangs from the line)
  • Quarter rest looks like a squiggly line
  • Eighth rest has a flag on the side

Learn how to tell the difference between rests by their appearance and remember them with helpful tricks.

A trick to tell the difference between a half and whole rest is that a half rest looks like a hat! The whole rest looks like a hole in the ground - it hangs down from the line. The quarter rest looks like a squiggly line or lightning bolt. The eighth rest has a small flag on the side and looks like the number 7.
