Recap

  • Hold the violin with your left hand
  • Rest the violin on your left shoulder
  • Place the chin rest under your jaw
  • Keep the violin level with the floor

Learn the proper way to hold a violin, including hand placement and posture for comfortable playing.

Hi! Welcome to lesson 1.2, holding the violin. The violin is most commonly held in our left hand and placed on our left shoulder. It’s very important when we put the violin on our shoulder that we don’t put it too high up or too far down, rather perfectly in the middle like this where the violin is level with the ground. When playing the violin, our head should be as upright as possible. We don’t want to put our cheek into the chin rest, like this, you see how my head is sideways? We should have our chin placed directly into the chin rest like this and our head pointing upright. To set up the left hand of the violin, we place our thumb on the side of the violin neck on the very first tape. We take our pointer finger and point it behind the tape like this. The final step is to curl the pointer finger over and press directly on the first tape. Your first tape is green, your second tape is blue, and your third tape should be red. This aligns with three fingers on the left hand. Where the first finger places on the green tape, two fingers places on the blue tape, and three fingers places on the red tape.
