Recap

  • Hold the violin with your left hand
  • Rest the violin on your left shoulder
  • Place the chin rest under your jaw
  • Keep the violin level with the floor

Learn the proper way to hold a violin, including hand placement and posture for comfortable playing.

The violin is most commonly held with the left hand. The violin should rest solidly on the player's left shoulder, settled where the chin rest is right under the player's jaw. The violin should be held level with the floor, not pointing down, and not pointing up.

Recap

  • Three finger tapes on the fingerboard
  • Pointer finger = first finger (first tape)
  • Middle finger = second finger (second tape)
  • Ring finger = third finger (third tape)

Learn about finger placement using tapes on the fingerboard to help guide your fingers to the correct positions.

In the beginning, we have three tapes that are played with the fingers of our left hand. Our pointer finger is considered the first finger, it presses on the first tape! Our middle finger is our second finger, it presses on the second tape. Our ring finger is our third finger, it presses on the third tape! Eventually you may have four tapes, but for these lessons, we'll focus on the first three.
