Recap

  • Written as two stacked numbers at the beginning of a line
  • The top number shows how many beats are in each measure
  • The bottom number tells us what type of note aligns with the beat

Explore how time signatures organize rhythm in music, including how they indicate the number of beats in each measure and which type of note receives one beat.

A time signature is used to tell us how to count our measure. It’s typically shown by placing two numbers on top of each other. It's placed at the beginning of our line. The top number tells us how many beats occur each measure. This can range from 2 to 12! The bottom number tells us what type of note aligns with the beat. A four on the bottom means quarter note, a two on the bottom means half note, an eight on the bottom means eighth note, a sixteen on the bottom means sixteenth note. For example: a time signature of 3 / 4 means that there are three beats in each measure and the quarter note aligns with the beat. A time signature of 6 / 8 means that there are six beats in a measure and the eighth note aligns with the beat.
