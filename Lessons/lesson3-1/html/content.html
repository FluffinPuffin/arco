Recap

  • Sharp (#) makes a note a half step higher
  • Flat (♭) makes a note a half step lower
  • A whole step would be a new note (B -> C)
  • A half step adds a sharp or flat (B -> B#)

Learn about sharps and flats - symbols that raise or lower notes by a half step.

Each note can be sharp (#) or flat (♭). These symbols make the note a half step higher or lower. A whole step higher would be a new note, like B to C. However, a half step would be B to B#. [Image of a staff showing the difference between whole and half steps]
