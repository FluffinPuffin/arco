Recap

  • D major scale begins on a D note
  • Follows the major scale pattern until the next D
  • D major notes: D, E, F#, G, A, B, C#, D
  • The scale has two sharps: F# and C#

Learn the D major scale - one of the most important scales for violin players.

(0:18 - 0:23) Now I want to demonstrate what a D major scale sounds like.
(0:23 - 0:39) The notes in a D major scale are D, E, F sharp, G, A, B, C sharp, and D. So notice that we started on D and we ended on D. That looks like this.
(0:39 - 0:45) So we make our way through the musical alphabet until we reach another D.
(0:45 - 0:53) If this were a G major scale we would start on G and make your way through the musical alphabet until you encounter another G.
