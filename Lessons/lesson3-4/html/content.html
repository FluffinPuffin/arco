Recap

  • A scale is a set of notes from one note to the next of the same name
  • Example: open D to high D
  • Scales follow a specific pattern of steps
  • Major scales are the most common type

Learn what a scale is and how it forms the foundation of music.

(0:03 - 0:08) Welcome to lesson 3.4 where we talk about what is a scale.
(0:08 - 0:17) A scale in music is a pattern of notes from one starting note to another of the same name.
(0:17 - 0:25) For example, if I start on the note D, I'm going to play through the musical alphabet until I reach another D.
(0:25 - 0:33) Major scales follow a specific pattern of whole steps and half steps to create this unique sound.
(0:33 - 0:39) We talked about half steps in a previous lesson, in this one we need to talk about whole steps as well.
(0:39 - 0:46) A half steps is the distance between notes that is small. Between C sharp and D for example.
(0:46 - 0:54) A whole step just means a slightly larger distance between notes. Like the distance between B and C sharp for example.
(0:53 - 1:03) A half step often sounds small and pinched like this. (plays violin).
(1:03 - 1:13) A whole step is going to sound more round and full like this. (plays violin).
(1:15 - 1:22) The reason this is important is because the pattern that major scales follow uses whole steps and half steps.
(1:23 - 1:41) This is the pattern of a major scale. It follows the pattern whole step, whole step, half step, whole step, whole step, another whole step and then a half step. Following this pattern creates a specific major scale pattern.
