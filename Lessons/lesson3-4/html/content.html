Recap

  • A scale is a set of notes from one note to the next of the same name
  • Example: open D to high D
  • Scales follow a specific pattern of steps
  • Major scales are the most common type

Learn what a scale is and how it forms the foundation of music.

A scale is a set of notes beginning at one note and ending at the next note of the same name. For example, from open D to high D on the violin. Scales follow specific patterns that give them their unique sound. Major scales are the most common type of scale you'll learn.
