Recap

  • The bow is held in the right hand
  • All five fingers work to hold the bow
  • Thumb and middle finger create a circle
  • Pinky sits rounded on top of the stick

Learn the proper technique for holding a violin bow with all five fingers working together.

The bow is most commonly held in the right hand. All five fingers work to hold the bow securely. The thumb and middle finger meet to create a circle. This circle is placed in the gap found in the frog. The pointer finger rests on the grip, lightly hooked for stability. The ring finger wraps over and covers the eye. The pinky is rounded, and sits right on top of the bow stick.
